Emilio Martinez Poppe| Ways of Being
Memorial Hall | Taproom
Details
Ways of Being is a free resource for educators that can be used in classroom, exhibition, and workshop contexts to analyze and reimagine power relationships in the arts. The textbook, syllabi, website, and videos are tools that prompt investigations into systems of production: how projects are made, how projects circulate, and how expressions of power are encoded in a wide range of approaches to production. We believe that an education in art must be as much about ways of being in the world as it is about ways of seeing and ways of making and exhibiting projects in the world.
Barbara Weinreich | "Building" a Brand from Bricks to Bytes
The MET | Metroom A
Details
A strong brand narrative informs its built form as a retail store. But how do you build a brand story? A series of retail design case studies will explore how some leading brands architecturally express their DNA. How does retail design extend-or even invent- the brand story? How does this traditional store approach relate to retail design today, as the model shifts from digital to drones to duopolies? What are emerging trends in retail design and how does customer desire, behavior and experience affect the future design of the brand story?
Design for America | Synthesize with Empathy
Ewing Multicultural Center
Details
Thoughtful synthesis, while not explicit in a solution's implementation, is nonetheless an essential step in informing a design intervention. It enables the designer to capture and process the mass of knowledge obtained from user research, and to identify underlying, implicit user needs. This workshop will introduce several synthesis methods and provide opportunities to practice and discuss on a smaller scale.

RISD Museum | Chace Metcalf Auditorium
Details
There is a great and growing imperative for innovation. It calls for more people to be more innovative more often. Beth Rattner | Nature as a storehouse of knowledge, not a warehouse of goods
RISD Auditorium
Details
Biomimicry (also “bio-inspired design”) is an approach to innovation that leads to sustainable design solutions by studying and applying nature’s functional strategies and patterns. At a time when many innovators have a fractured, if any, relationship to nature, biomimicry offers a way to reconnect. It is a profound shift in how we view and value the natural world. How might a designer or engineer newly understand lightweighting and aerodynamics by studying how a hummingbird cross the Gulf of Mexico on minimal body fat? Beth's presentation will be both a 30 minute talk and a brief, interactive example of how to apply the biomimicry design process. The overview will include case studies that focus on phenomena right outside in one’s own backyard and striking examples from nature and bio-inspired design that are tied directly to biology, chemistry, physics, environmental science, and engineering at the high school level. As biomimicry is a multi-disciplinary approach -- synthesizing biology, engineering, design, math -- attendees of the talk will see that collaboration, innovation, and critical thinking are by-products of this lens. At the same time, an undeniable sense of wonder and hope are sparked, not just from the innate desire we all have to observe nature, but from understanding that solutions are all around us, all the time. In this way, biomimicry is a "block" upon which a new kind of design practice is possible, and a "byte" in that it abstracts design principles from nature's blueprints and applies it to our imperfect, industrial materials palette. The selected case studies feature students and entrepreneurs worldwide who have designed nature-inspired solutions to critical sustainability problems through our Biomimicry Global Design Challenge, focused on climate change.

Join us for a Creative Jam!

Please join us for a Creative Jam, an inspiring two-part design experience that demonstrates and celebrates creativity.

The Adobe Creative Cloud team will host the Creative Jam presentations. This one-hour design showcase highlights the work of local creative leaders.

At the same time, in the next room, design teams will be competing in the Creative Jam tournament, a 3-hour design showdown. This event will have several categories: motion design, graphic design and UX/UI. Once the Creative Jam presentations wrap, tournament participants will take the stage to share their creations with you. As part of the audience, you’ll vote on each submission and select the People's Choice winner. There will also be Grand Prize winners decided by a group of Judges!

Visit https://nvite.com/CreativeJam/betterxdesign for more details.
